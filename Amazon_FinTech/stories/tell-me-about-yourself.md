## Main script (say this)

I've always been drawn to building things from scratch, especially in settings where there's more unknown than known. Early in my career, that meant developing a deep, rigorous understanding of complex problems—sometimes using mathematical tools, sometimes using machine learning—because when the problem is ill-defined, intuition alone isn't enough. I learned to rely on first principles, careful experimentation, and a willingness to revise assumptions quickly.

Over time, my expertise concentrated around sequential modeling, representation learning, and generative and multimodal models. I've applied these ideas across a wide range of problems, which led to publishing 40+ papers and carrying projects from an initial hypothesis all the way to validated outcomes—the hypothesis, implementation, training, and eval were work I did hands-on. What matters most to me, though, is not the publication itself, but seeing ideas turn into systems that actually work.

As my career progressed, I found it increasingly rewarding to move closer to impact—taking theory and turning it into practical, scalable solutions. That shift is what excites me about Amazon. It's an environment where ambiguity is expected, where you have to invent your way to clarity, and where strong technical judgment and execution really matter. The opportunity to do that at scale—building solutions that move from concept to real customer value—is exactly the direction I want to continue growing in.
